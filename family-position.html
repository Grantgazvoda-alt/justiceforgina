THE GAZVODA FAMILY'S STATED POSITION

Why Gina's sons believe her death requires a potential-homicide investigation.

Grant and Garrison Gazvoda believe Nicholas Forte may have caused, contributed to, or concealed circumstances related to Gina Gazvoda's death. They base that belief on the cumulative record—not one isolated document—and ask law enforcement and prosecutors to test their theory through an independent investigation.

Important:This is an attributed allegation and family belief. Nicholas Forte has not been convicted of causing Gina's death, and this website does not present the family's theory as an established fact.

THE THEORY IN PLAIN LANGUAGE

The family believes the official explanation is incomplete.

The family points to reported conflict and distress before Gina's death, disputed descriptions of her condition, requests for medical care, contradictions in the day-of-death timeline, the lack of an autopsy, rapid cremation, death-document concerns, estate disputes, and later forgery-related proceedings. The family believes those issues should be examined together by authorities with subpoena power and access to complete records.

What this page does not claim: It does not claim that every family statement is independently verified, that every disputed document is fraudulent, or that the published material alone proves criminal causation.

EVIDENCE CATEGORIES

Why the family reached its belief.

01

Days before Gina's death

Family witnesses report emotional distress, relationship conflict, rapidly changing descriptions of Gina's condition, and requests that she receive medical care.

02

The final-day timeline

The family says reported arrival times, calls, witness accounts, and later recollections do not align cleanly with the official incident narrative.

03

Medical response and no autopsy

The family questions why Gina was not taken for emergency evaluation, why the medical examiner declined the case, and why an autopsy was not performed despite the sons' stated request.

04

Cremation and death documentation

The family disputes the authority and process used for cremation and raises questions about who supplied information used in death documentation.

05

Estate and beneficiary documents

The family has challenged wills, signatures, beneficiary or account designations, and related probate events. Expert material and pending forgery-related proceedings are presented separately from the death theory.

06

Changed or conflicting accounts

The family says multiple descriptions of Gina's health, events, and timing changed over time and should be tested against phone, location, medical, police, EMS, and witness records.

QUESTIONS FOR AUTHORITIES

What an independent investigation should determine.

RECORDS

Reconstruct the final 72 hours

  • Obtain complete phone and message records.
  • Compare witness accounts and arrival times.
  • Review available images, video, and metadata.
MEDICAL

Reassess cause and manner

  • Review complete medical and home-care records.
  • Evaluate the decision not to perform an autopsy.
  • Obtain independent forensic and clinical opinions.
DOCUMENTS

Trace authority and financial changes

  • Authenticate disputed signatures and forms.
  • Identify who prepared and submitted key documents.
  • Determine whether financial motive or concealment is supported.
