GINA'S STORY

More than a case file.

Gina Gazvoda was a mother and a loved member of her family. Her death left grief, unanswered questions, and years of legal and investigative proceedings.

This project exists to preserve her memory while building a clear, chronological, source-based public record. It is not a substitute for a court, law-enforcement agency, medical expert, or independent journalist. It is a structured place where the available records and the family's account can be reviewed responsibly.

“We are asking people to examine the documents, follow the timeline, and help us pursue the truth through lawful channels.”

FREQUENTLY ASKED

Clarity matters.

Does this website claim anyone murdered Gina?

No. The family has serious concerns and is seeking further review, but this site does not present an unproven homicide allegation as an established fact. Criminal responsibility can be determined only through lawful investigation and court proceedings.

Does the arrest prove the forgery charges?

No. Arrest and charging records establish that accusations are pending; they do not establish guilt. The accused is presumed innocent unless proven guilty.

Why are some documents not public yet?

Materials must be authenticated, organized, reviewed for privacy, redacted where needed, and checked for lawful publication. Medical, personal, privileged, sealed, or third-party-sensitive records may remain restricted.

How can the public help?

Share verified case updates, refer qualified journalists or attorneys, connect the family with reputable advocacy organizations, and support the fundraiser after reviewing it independently. Do not threaten, harass, contact, or publish private information about any person involved.
