OFFICIAL RECORDS · PRIVACY-REDACTED COMPARISON

Police and EMS recorded different explanations for the reporting witness's absence that day.

The police narrative says the reporting witness stated that he left at approximately 6:00 a.m. for work. The EMS narrative says he stated that he left that morning to go fishing. Both accounts appear in official response records concerning the same death.

What this establishes—and what it does not.The records establish that the two agency narratives documented different explanations. They do not establish why the accounts differ, whether either summary is complete, or whether the difference reflects criminal conduct.

SIDE-BY-SIDE SOURCE REVIEW

Two official narratives. One unresolved difference.

POLICE NARRATIVE

Work account

The police report records the reporting witness as stating that he left the residence at approximately 6:00 a.m. to go to work and returned around 7:00 p.m.

Source class: Police-generated incident narrative.

EMS NARRATIVE

Fishing account

The EMS report records the reporting witness as stating that he left that morning to go fishing, attempted telephone calls later in the day, and returned home before calling for help.

Source class: EMS-generated patient-care narrative.

UNRESOLVED

Reason for the difference

The public records reviewed do not resolve whether the difference arose from separate statements, note-taking, later clarification, misunderstanding, or another cause.

Required review: Audio, complete witness interviews, original notes, call records, and sworn testimony.

SOURCE AND REDACTION NOTE

How this page was prepared.

Primary source: A combined Waterbury police and EMS report preserved in the Gazvoda family evidence archive. The archive copy contains personal identifiers and medical details that are not necessary to understand this discrepancy. This public page omits exact addresses, birth dates, signatures, private contact information, and nonessential medical information. The untouched source remains preserved for qualified legal, journalistic, and investigative review.
Verification status: The existence and wording of the two narratives were directly compared in the archived report. The Justice for Gina team has not independently determined why the narratives differ. Readers should not treat this comparison as a finding that any person lied or committed a crime.
