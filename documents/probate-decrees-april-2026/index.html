CONNECTICUT PROBATE COURT · SIGNED DECREES

The Probate Court invalidated a will based on fraud and removed Nicholas Forte as executor.

Two decrees signed by Waterbury Probate Judge Matthew P. Vaccarelli on April 22, 2026 address the Estate of Gina Marie Gazvoda, case number 21-01204.

Scope of the court findings:The decrees establish rulings about the July 28, 2019 will and estate administration. They do not establish homicide, cause of death, or that the will-related fraud proves responsibility for Gina's death.

DECREE ONE · WILL CONTEST

July 28, 2019 will revoked and invalidated.

COURT FINDING

Parties stipulated the will was fraudulent

The decree states that Grant E. Gazvoda and Garrison Gazvoda challenged the validity of the original will dated July 28, 2019, which had previously been admitted to probate.

ORDERED AND DECREED

Will invalidated based on fraud

The court ordered that the July 28, 2019 will be revoked and invalidated based on fraud pursuant to Connecticut General Statutes section 45a-251.

LIMITATION

No death-causation ruling

The decree resolves the will contest described in the document. It does not identify who created any false document, decide criminal guilt, or address how Gina died.

DECREE TWO · ESTATE ADMINISTRATION

Nicholas Forte removed as executor.

01

Removal

The court removed Nicholas Forte as executor after the April 22, 2026 hearing.

02

Successor administrator

The court appointed Attorney Edward Duffy as administrator of the estate.

03

Financial report

The decree ordered Nicholas Forte to file a final financial report no later than May 31, 2026.

04

Inventory

The decree ordered the newly appointed administrator to file an inventory within 60 days of the decree.
