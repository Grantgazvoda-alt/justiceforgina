02

Phone-record assertions

The draft states that calls and messages occurred at specific times. Those entries should be treated as record-dependent until the underlying carrier records, device logs, screenshots, or authenticated exports are reviewed.

03

Attributed recollections

The chronology records what family members and other witnesses reportedly remembered about calls, arrival order, statements, and observations. Each account belongs to the named speaker and is not independently verified merely by appearing in the timeline.

04

Travel estimates and assumptions

The document estimates travel using no-traffic map results taken around 1:00 a.m. and assumes preparation time before departure. Those estimates are illustrative, not historical traffic reconstruction or location evidence.

05

Family inference

The concluding section argues that reported arrival sequences and statements do not fit together. That is the family's inference and request for investigation—not an established factual or criminal conclusion.

CLAIM-TESTING REQUIREMENTS

What would be needed to turn working entries into reliable findings.

COMMUNICATIONS

Authenticated call and message records

Carrier logs, device records, timestamps, metadata, and complete message context should be matched to the people and devices involved.

LOCATION AND TRAVEL

Historical evidence—not generic maps

Reliable reconstruction would require available location records, toll or marina records, receipts, images with metadata, contemporaneous traffic information, and testimony—not modern no-traffic estimates alone.

WITNESS ACCOUNTS

Separate interviews and corroboration

Each reported recollection should be recorded in the witness's own words, dated, compared with prior statements, and checked against independent records.

EVENT SEQUENCE

Authority-led reconstruction

Only investigators with access to complete records, original evidence, and witness interviews can reliably determine who was notified, who arrived when, and what occurred before the emergency call.

WHAT THIS WORKING TIMELINE DOES NOT ESTABLISH

Chronology is not causation.

No verified departure or return reconstruction: The reviewed family document does not independently prove the time, route, or purpose of any person's travel.
No verified arrival order: Reported arrival times and who was present require agency records, witness interviews, communications, and other contemporaneous evidence.
No medical or criminal determination: The timeline does not determine when Gina died, the cause or manner of death, or whether any person committed a crime.
Canonical public version: The site's web-native timeline and source-specific official pages supersede the private working document for public use.
