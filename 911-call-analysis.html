FBI-INFORMED METHOD

Compare statements to facts—not emotion to guilt

The review framework follows the cautious principle described in the FBI Law Enforcement Bulletin: verbal observations are most useful when compared with case facts, written statements, and later interviews. Tone, crying, pitch, pauses, or emotional presentation cannot independently establish deception.

Use

Statement-to-record comparison

Timestamp the caller’s exact words and compare them with official dispatch, police, EMS, telephone, and sworn records.

Use cautiously

Observable language

Record volunteered explanations, corrections, sequence changes, minimization, and references to others—but do not convert a language feature into a finding of guilt.

Do not use

Voice-stress “lie detection”

The project will not use automated voice-stress software or claim that vocal stress proves deception. National Institute of Justice testing has found serious accuracy limitations.
