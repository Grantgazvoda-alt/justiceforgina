REVIEW QUESTIONS

What the transcript and records must test

The following are disputed questions raised by family-created and witness-attributed records. Public release of the audio does not resolve them automatically.

A family-created witness timeline reports a conflict concerning whether anyone else was present when responders needed access. Background audio must be identified conservatively and corroborated independently.

Rule: An unidentified voice will not be assigned to a person without corroboration.

UNRESOLVEDSource comparison

CPR instructions and activity

Family materials dispute whether CPR was actually performed. Review must separate dispatcher instructions, the caller’s responses, audible activity, EMS documentation, and witness recollections.

Rule: The site will not describe CPR as fake or staged without reliable source and expert support.

UNRESOLVEDChronology review

Prior movements and timing

Statements about earlier movements must be compared with the police report, EMS record, dispatch timestamps, phone records, and later sworn testimony. A discrepancy is a documented question—not automatic proof of intent or guilt.

FBI-INFORMED METHOD

Compare statements to facts—not emotion to guilt

The review framework follows the cautious principle described in the FBI Law Enforcement Bulletin: verbal observations are most useful when compared with case facts, written statements, and later interviews. Tone, crying, pitch, pauses, or emotional presentation cannot independently establish deception.

Use

Statement-to-record comparison

Timestamp the caller’s exact words and compare them with official dispatch, police, EMS, telephone, and sworn records.

Use cautiously

Observable language

Record volunteered explanations, corrections, sequence changes, minimization, and references to others—but do not convert a language feature into a finding of guilt.

Do not use

Voice-stress “lie detection”

The project will not use automated voice-stress software or claim that vocal stress proves deception. National Institute of Justice testing has found serious accuracy limitations.

Reliability limit: This project does not use a checklist of supposedly “guilty” words, emotions, pauses, or reactions to decide whether a caller is truthful. The recording is treated as a contemporaneous statement whose exact contents must be tested against independent evidence. Behavioral resemblance to another call is not forensic proof.

COMPARATIVE CASE CONTROL

The Murdaugh call may illustrate categories—not prove this case

A later comparison may examine observable categories such as scene description, volunteered chronology, references to other people, emergency instructions, and changes between the call and later accounts. Similarity between two calls does not establish the same act, motive, or guilt.

Publication limit: No substantive side-by-side conclusion will be published until Gina’s call has a reliable timestamped transcript and the comparison uses a primary recording or authenticated transcript for each call.
